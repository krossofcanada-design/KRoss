Crisis Lines & Urgent Help

If you or someone you know is in danger, can’t stay safe, or feels overwhelmed — you are not alone. Help is available 24/7, free, and confidential.

Canada-wide Crisis Support:

Kids Help Phone: Call 1-800-668-6868 or text CONNECT to 686868. For kids, teens and young adults from coast to coast.

9-8-8 Suicide Crisis Helpline: Call or text 9-8-8 — available Canada-wide.

If it’s an emergency: Call 911 or go to the nearest hospital emergency department.

You deserve help right now — you don’t need to wait until things get worse.

It’s totally normal not to know where to begin. Reaching out can feel big, but you don’t have to have the “right words” — you just have to start talking.

You can begin by:

  • Telling someone you trust — a friend, parent, teacher, coach, or elder: “Hey, I’ve been struggling lately. Can we talk?”
  • Seeing a school counselor or campus mental health worker if you’re in high school, college, or university.
  • Booking an appointment with a family doctor or nurse practitioner — they can connect you to local services.
  • Exploring online support if you’re not ready for face-to-face conversations.

Tip: It’s okay to try a few different types of support before finding what feels right.

Not everyone feels ready for counseling, and that’s okay — investigate these online resources to understand your possibilities and where to find the kind of help you need.

Canada-wide:

  • Kids Help Phone: For kids, teens, and young adults from coast to coast. Crisis support, access to a counsellor, as well as skill-building tools.
  • Jack.org: Youth-led mental health movement with chapters in most provinces and online talks and stories of recovery.
  • Canadian Mental Health Association (CMHA): National organization with provincial chapters. Go here if you want in-depth knowledge on mental health as well as an overview of how to build up a support team around you. This site also provides info on where to find help.
  • Youth Mental Health Canada (YMHC): Educator-based platform working to provide essential resources to schools, families, and health care professionals. Explore this site to see what is available through your school.
  • Centre for Addiction and Mental Health (CAMH): National leader of research within mental health with mental health centres in Toronto. An in-depth website for when you have time and want to know about the newest research.

    • By Province:

    NB

    • Integrated Youth Services (IYS): Cross-sector youth hubs for ages 12–25 that integrate mental health, physical health, education support, peer programs, and family services. A collaboration between the Government of New Brunswick and CMHA-NB.
    • Atlantic Wellness: Free, professional therapy for youth aged 12–21 with no limit on sessions. Available both in-person and virtually, offering one of the province’s few low-barrier mental-health services.
    • Bridge the Gap: Low-barrier school-based and online mental health support helping youth connect with appropriate community services.
    • Horizon Health – Child & Youth Mental Health Services: Multidisciplinary teams of psychologists, social workers, and nurses providing support in clinics and directly within school environments.
    • Dots NB (Partners for Youth): Youth-led programming and school campaigns aimed at reducing stigma around mental health.
    • Imprint Youth Association & ConneQT NB: Safe social spaces, counselling, and targeted support for LGBTQIA2S+ youth across the province.
    • Harvest House: Moncton-based organization offering individual counselling and peer-support programs.
    • Wellness on Wheels: A mobile initiative bringing curated mental health toolkits, workshops, and resources to Southeastern New Brunswick.

    Peer-to-peer support reminds you that healing happens in community, not isolation.
